FAQ

Common Questions About
Payroll in Egypt

+What are the employer social insurance rates in Egypt?

Employers contribute 18.75% of gross salary to NOSI. Employees contribute 11%. Total social insurance is 29.75%. Staffona calculates, remits, and files all NOSI contributions on your behalf every month.

+What is Egypt's income tax rate for 2026?

Egypt uses a progressive income tax: 0% up to EGP 40,000 annual income, 10% up to EGP 55,000, 15% up to EGP 70,000, 20% up to EGP 200,000, 22.5% up to EGP 400,000, and 25% above EGP 400,000.

+Can Staffona handle payroll in multiple currencies?

Yes. Payroll is processed in EGP for Egyptian regulatory purposes. Client invoices are issued in USD, AED, GBP, or EUR as preferred. We handle all exchange rate conversions.

+What happens if an employee joins or leaves mid-month?

Pro-rated salaries are calculated based on working days. NOSI registration or de-registration is handled immediately. No additional fees for mid-month changes.

+What is Form 4 and when is it due?

Form 4 (Nموذج 4) is Egypt's quarterly salary return submitted to the Egyptian Tax Authority. It reports all employee salaries and tax withheld for the quarter. Staffona prepares and submits it quarterly — always on time.

+Do I need a local entity to use Staffona's payroll service?

No. If you don't have an Egyptian entity, Staffona can provide the payroll service as part of our managed employment (EOR) offering — we become the legal employer and handle all payroll, NOSI, and tax on your behalf. Learn about managed employment →
