Complete Guide to
Hiring in Egypt

Everything you need to legally hire employees in Egypt ‚Äî from finding candidates to running your first compliant payroll.

Egypt offers one of the most competitive and talented workforces in the region. But hiring here involves several legal steps that, if missed, can lead to significant compliance penalties. This guide walks you through the complete process.

Step 1 ‚Äî Understand the Legal Framework

All private sector employment in Egypt is governed by Labor Law No. 12 of 2003. This law covers employment contracts, working hours, leave entitlements, termination procedures, and employee rights. Additionally, Social Insurance Law 148/2019 mandates employer and employee contributions to the National Organization for Social Insurance (NOSI).

If you are a foreign company without an Egyptian legal entity, you cannot directly hire Egyptian employees. You must either register a legal entity in Egypt, or use a managed employment provider like Staffona to employ staff on your behalf ‚Äî legally and compliantly.

Step 2 ‚Äî Prepare the Employment Contract

Egyptian law requires that all employment contracts be written in Arabic (or bilingual Arabic/English). The contract must be registered with the Labor Office. Key items to include:

  • Employee full name, national ID, and address
  • Job title and full description of duties
  • Start date and contract duration (fixed or indefinite)
  • Salary, bonus structure, and payment terms
  • Working hours, leave entitlements, and probation period
  • Termination conditions and notice period

Contracts that are not in Arabic or not registered with the Labor Office are technically non-compliant. Staffona prepares all contracts in bilingual Arabic/English format and handles all registrations.

Step 3 ‚Äî Register with the Social Insurance Authority (NOSI)

Every employer operating in Egypt must register with the National Organization for Social Insurance (NOSI) and enroll each employee within 30 days of hiring. Contributions must be paid monthly:

  • Employer contribution: 18.75% of the employee's insurable wage
  • Employee contribution: 11% of the insurable wage

Failure to register or remit contributions on time results in penalties. NOSI contributions fund pensions, disability benefits, and death benefits for employees.

Step 4 ‚Äî Set Up Payroll

Egyptian payroll requires monthly calculation of gross salary, deduction of employee social insurance (11%), deduction of income tax (progressive rates from 0% to 25%), and net pay calculation. The employer must also:

  • Withhold and remit income tax to the Egyptian Tax Authority monthly
  • Remit both employer and employee NOSI contributions monthly
  • Submit quarterly Form 4 salary returns
  • Conduct annual tax reconciliation

Payroll must be processed in Egyptian Pounds (EGP), though your service agreement with employees may include USD or other currency equivalents.

Step 5 ‚Äî Register with the Labor Office

Employers must notify the local Labor Office of new hires. This registration is required by law and is separate from NOSI registration. The employment contract must be submitted as part of this process.

Step 6 ‚Äî Ongoing Compliance

Once hired, ongoing compliance requires:

  • Monthly payroll processing and tax/NOSI remittances
  • Quarterly Form 4 salary return filings
  • Annual income tax reconciliation
  • Updating contracts when Egyptian law changes (minimum wage, tax brackets)
  • Maintaining employee records for audit purposes
