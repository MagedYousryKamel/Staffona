Payroll Taxes in Egypt
Complete Employer Guide

Income tax brackets, social insurance rates, quarterly Form 4 returns, and employer filing obligations ‚Äî updated for 2026.

Egyptian payroll involves two main statutory deductions: income tax and social insurance. Employers must calculate, withhold, and remit both every month ‚Äî and file quarterly returns. Here's everything you need to know.

1. Income Tax (Salary Tax)

Egyptian income tax is progressive, calculated on annual gross salary. Employers must withhold the applicable monthly amount and remit it to the Egyptian Tax Authority (ETA) each month.

2. Social Insurance (NOSI)

Under Social Insurance Law 148/2019, both employer and employee contribute to the National Organization for Social Insurance (NOSI) monthly:

The "insurable wage" is capped at a maximum set by NOSI (updated periodically). Contributions fund pensions, work injury, unemployment, and health benefits.

3. Quarterly Form 4 Returns

Every quarter, employers must submit a Form 4 salary return to the Egyptian Tax Authority. This declares all employee salaries, tax withheld, and any adjustments for the quarter. Deadlines:

4. Annual Tax Reconciliation

At the end of each tax year, employers must reconcile all salary tax withheld against annual calculations. Any underpayment must be settled; any overpayment credited or refunded. Staffona handles annual reconciliation for all managed employees.

5. National Minimum Wage

Egypt's national minimum wage is set by the National Wage Council and updated periodically by Presidential Decree. As of 2026, the minimum wage for private sector employees is EGP 6,000 per month. All Staffona-managed salaries comply with the current minimum.
